Doppler Effect
The Doppler effect is the change in frequency of a wave for an observer moving relative to its source. It is commonly heard when a vehicle sounding a siren or horn approaches, passes, and recedes from an observer. The received frequency is higher during the approach (compared to the emitted frequency), it is identical at the instant of passing by, and it is lower during the recession.
